Hello!

I'm Duc Vu, an undergraduate researcher in Computer Engineering at Michigan State University and a former summer research intern at Carnegie Mellon University.

My primary research lies at the intersection of graph signal processing and neuroimaging, where I focus on developing learning-based methods for brain networks by leveraging hypergraph theory and graph neural networks. I'm also interested in designing wearable sensing systems with applications in biomedical domains.

🧪 Selected Projects

Processing Pipeline for HCP Data

Developed a high-throughput SLURM-based pipeline for processing Human Connectome Project data, enabling parallelized execution of intensive tasks like FreeSurfer’s recon-all and Glasser parcellation.

Graph High-Pass Neural Networks

Implemented a GNN using a Chebyshev rational high-pass filter kernel to scale spectral graph processing to large datasets without costly eigendecomposition.

VergeIO — EOG-based smart glasses enabling depth-aware interaction

I created a video to showcase VergeIO shortly after our paper submission. The video provides a concise demonstration of our core innovations and potential applications. It is narrated by Professor Justin Chan and serves as an accessible introduction to the system’s design and capabilities.
